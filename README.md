# Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn data for a subscription-based technology company to identify factors influencing customer retention and provide business recommendations.

## Business Objective

The objective was to understand why customers leave and identify opportunities to improve retention and customer lifetime value.

## Dataset Information

- Records: 7,043 customers
- Features: 21 columns
- Tools: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Google Colab, GitHub

## Data Cleaning

- Converted TotalCharges from text to numeric format.
- Identified and removed 11 missing values.
- Verified data quality before analysis.

## Key Findings

### 1. Churn Rate
- 26.6% of customers churned.

### 2. Contract Type
- Month-to-month churn rate: 42.7%
- One-year churn rate: 11.3%
- Two-year churn rate: 2.8%

### 3. Monthly Charges
- Churned customers pay £74.44/month on average.
- Retained customers pay £61.31/month on average.

### 4. Customer Tenure
- Churned customers stay for 18 months on average.
- Retained customers stay for 38 months on average.

### 5. Internet Service
- Fiber Optic customers have a 41.9% churn rate.
- DSL customers have a 19.0% churn rate.

## Business Recommendations

1. Encourage customers to move to longer-term contracts.
2. Improve onboarding and engagement during the first 18 months.
3. Review pricing strategies for higher-paying customers.
4. Investigate Fiber Optic customer experience and service quality.
5. Implement targeted retention campaigns for at-risk customers.
